Broadcom Inc
Solid Results/Guidance on Al Semi Upside; Expects
$100B+ in Al Chip Sales In FY27 On 10 GW Of
Compute Capacity Across An Expanding Customer
Base; GM Expectations Tick Up; Reit OW
Broadcom reported better-than-expected results for the January quarter and
provided a solid revenue outlook for the April quarter, driven by continued upside
in the Al semiconductor segment. This strength aligns with what we've seen in the
TPU 3nm Ironwood program and Al networking. The company reported $8.4B in
Al revenues (up ~ 30% Q/Q and ~ 105% Y/Y) for the January quarter and expects
this to increase to $10.7B in the April quarter (up 27% Q/Q and~140% Y/Y), ahead
of consensus and in line with our preview expectations (see note here), where we
anticipated total revenue guidance of $21-22B and $10-11B in AI revenues.
Notably, AI networking accounted for one-third of total AI revenues in Q1 and is
expected to increase to 40% of total AI revenue in Q2, implying that revenues
should grow 50%+ Q/Q. We believe this performance puts Broadcom on track to
deliver over $65B in AI revenues in FY26. More importantly, visibility into 2027
has dramatically improved, with the team now having line of sight to achieving
$100B+ in Al chip revenues in FY27 (we previewed >$120B). Broadcom
anticipates close to 10 GW of total compute capacity being deployed in FY27
across its six XPU customers. Specifically, OpenAl has now become a qualified
customer and is expected to deploy over one gigawatt of compute capacity in
FY27. Anthropic's order deployment is expected to increase 3x Y/Y in FY27 to 3
GW. Meta remains on track with its internal ASIC program, with multiple
gigawatts expected to be deployed in FY27. Customers 4 and 5 (we believe to be
SoftBank/Arm and Bytedance) are expected to have strong shipments this year and
more than double in 2027. All of this is in addition to the continued strength of
Broadcom's lead customer and the ramp of Google's TPU v8 (Sunfish) program.
Overall, we estimate Broadcom can drive $12-15B of revenue per gigawatt and
conservatively see Al revenues at $120B+ in FY27. The team has fully secured
capacity (from leading-edge wafers, HBM, substrates) to support customer
buildouts from 2026 through 2028. On the margin side, the team is not seeing any
gross margin degradation as it ramps up XPU program for Anthropic in the 2H of
the year— and that alleviates a major investor concern and overhang for the stock.
As we had written about previously (see note here), the team is not seeing any share
impact from customer COT (in-house deisgn efforts) and doesn't expect any major
competitive dynamics for at least another few more years at the earliest. For non-AI
semiconductors, revenue growth remains stable and should provide additional
revenue and gross margin upside as end-market recovery gains momentum next
year. We are increasing our estimates and increasing our price target to $500 (from
$475). We reiterate our Overweight (OW) rating on AVGO, which remains our top
pick in the semiconductor sector.

• AI semis upside drove better-than-expected January quarter results. F1Q26
revenue was $19.31B, above consensus ($19.258B), driven by strong Al-related
semiconductor revenue, which came in at $8.4B (up 30% Q/Q and 105% Y/Y),
ahead of Street expectations of $8. 1B. Non-Al semiconductor revenue declined 11%
Q/Q (flattish Y/Y) to S4.11B due to seasonal softness in Wireless. Software
infrastructure revenues were $6.8B (down 2% Q/Q and up 1% Y/Y), largely in line
with expectations. Gross margin decreased ~90bps Q/Q to 77.0%, in line with
consensus. EPS of $2.05 was slightly above consensus ($2.03).
• April quarter revenue guidance well ahead of expectations, driven by continued
XPU strength and accelerating Al networking demand. April quarter revenue
was guided to $22.0B, above consensus ($20.53B), with Al semiconductor revenue
again being the key driver of sequential growth for AVGO. AI revenue is expected
to increase 27% Q/Q to approximately $10.7B for the quarter (up ~140% Y/Y),
reflecting accelerating growth in Al networking and continued strength in the ramp
of the TPU 3nm Ironwood program. AI networking accounted for one-third of total
Al revenues in Q1 and is expected to increase to 40% of total AI revenue in Q2,
implying that revenues should grow 50% Q/Q. Non-AI semiconductor revenue is
expected to remain flattish Q/Q at S4.1B (up 2% Y/Y). Infrastructure software was
guided to $7.2B, implying 6% Q/Q growth (up 9% Y/Y). Gross margin was guided
to remain flattish Q/Q at 77.0%.
• Expect the team to drive S65B in AI revenues in FY26 and S120B+ in
FY27..10GW of compute capacity deployed across an expanding customer
base. We expect Broadcom to generate close to $65B+ in AI (ASIC + networking)
revenues in FY26, led by its primary TPU customer, Anthropic ramp, the first-
generation ramp of SoftBank/ARM XPU ASIC programs, and incremental revenue
contributions from other AI ASIC customers such as Meta and Bytedance, along
with continued strong Al networking revenue. For FY27, Broadcom anticipates
close to 10 GW of total compute capacity being deployed across its six XPU
customers. Specifically, OpenAl has now become a qualified customer and is
expected to deploy over one gigawatt of compute capacity. Anthropic's order
deployment is projected to increase 3x Y/Y in FY27 to 3 GW. Meta remains on track
with its internal ASIC program, with multiple gigawatts expected to be deployed in
FY27. Customers 4 and 5 (which we believe to be SoftBank/ARM and Bytedance,
based on a process of elimination given Broadcom's disclosures) are expected to
have strong shipments this year and more than double in 2027. All of this is in
addition to the continued strength of Broadcom's lead customer and the ramp of the
TPU v8 (Sunfish) program. Overall, we estimate Broadcom can drive $12-15B of
revenue per gigawatt and conservatively see Al revenues at $120B+ in FY27.
Reflecting increasing AI customer diversification, we estimate Google TPU business
will decline to less than 45% of overall AI revenues in FY27, down from 50-52% in
FY26 and 60% in FY25—even as total AI revenues grow to over $120B in FY27.
Increasing FY26 estimates, initiating FY27 estimates, and raising our PT to
$500. We increase our FY26 estimates and with our new Al revenue outlook of
$120B in FY27, we are initiating FY27/CY27 EPS estimates of $19.61 and $20.45,
respectively. Our new price target of $500 (up from $475) assumes AVGO trades at
a 23-25x group multiple applied to our CY27 EPS estimate of $20.45.
