Summary Thoughts
Much of the call focused on AVGO's F2027 Al revenue guidance of >$100B, but this is
really only just in-line with our pre-call model of 5106B and most pre-call investor
bogeys in a similar range. We think the more incremental commentary for investors was
actually on margins where AVGO feels it can sustain current margin levels despite the
previously announced ~ $21B in Anthropic orders (which it had signaled would be racks
and therefore dilutive to margins). Having spoken to the company, we get the sense that
the mix of what it plans to ship to Anthropic is still evolving but may now include even
more high margin networking and potentially a pivot to using an ODM partner for any
racks that Anthropic still wants (but still almost all TPU). Net, the $100B still seems very
conservative to us and we are increasing our F2027 Al forecast to >$130B due to two
factors: 1) our supply chain work now suggesting AVGO's TPU unit shipments may be in
the range of ~6MM next year, and 2) commentary from management around 2027
shipments in GW terms (where we think AVGO's revenue per GW is actually in a similar
range to AMD's ~$15B). The combo of more Al revenue and less dilutive impact to
margins yields an increase in revs/EPS from ~$121B/$12.67 to ~$130B/$14.70 in
C2026E, from ~$167B/$18.05 to~$195B/$22.76 in C2027E, and from ~$211B/$22.59
to ~ $241B/$28.07 in C2028E. Despite these much higher estimates, we are leaving our
$475 PT unchanged as this now ascribes a ~21x P/E multiple for C2027E and ~ 17x for
C2028E - numbers which we feel more appropriately discount concerns around the
sustainability of Al spending levels (which are unlikely to lift in a meaningful way.
Analysis/Key Highlights
• FQ1 revenue of $19.3B came in above guidance and slightly above UBSe/Street
$19.1B/$19.3B. Gross margin of 77.0% was roughly in line with UBSe/Street, and
operating margin was ~80bps ahead at 66.4%, resulting in EPS of $2.05 - above
UBSe $1.99/Street $2.03. Al revenue was $200MM above guidance at $8.4B
driven by Custom ASIC. We estimate Al compute grew 28% Q/Q to $5.63B and Al
networking 34% Q/Q, to $2.77B
• FQ2 revenue guided to $22.0B - ahead of UBSe/Street at $20.8B/$20.5B led
largely by ~ $1.4B of upside to Al revenue guidance - which relative to our model is
split about evenly between TPU and networking (which is being led by very strong
demand for Tomahawk 6). Gross margins should be flattish QiQ and
EBITDA margin was guided to 68%, also flat Q/Q.

went after the narrative that agentic Al could push more workload to the cloud by

suggested that agentic Al will be good for VMWare because enterprises will want
to keep more sensitive data that would be the foundation for agentic workloads
on-premises rather than putting this into the cloud. Regardless, ve model growth
in the software business to decelerate and grow more in the mid single digit %
range versus VMW currently growing in the low double digits.

Read Across
We view AVGO's expectations of F2027 XPU revenue ramp across multiple customers as
a bullish sign on hyperscalers expectations for continued compute - should read
positively for Al compute names. A strong growth in networking reads positive for Al
networking players, like MRVL.
