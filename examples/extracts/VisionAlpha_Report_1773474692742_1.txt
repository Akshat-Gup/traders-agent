Thinking Through SOTP Amid Software Deep
Dive + EPS Preview
Summary
On the heels of our recent TPU-driven estimate increase and deeper dive into the
customer-owned-tooling debate for the Al business, we shift our focus to the software
business with a companion note from the UBS software team that highlights some
potential risks to growth. The recent correction in software multiples has, we think, been
a factor in AVGO's lagging stock performance. However, even adjusting for dis-
synergies in a hypothetical SOTP, our analysis suggests that AVGO's semiconductor
business is trading at 20x P/E, 23x EV/FCF, and 17x EV/EBITDA, all one turn more
expensive than NVDA (and the broader peer set). In other words, we are hard-pressed to
agree with prevailing investor sentiment that the semis business has become expensive
At the same time, though, the companion note highlights some key emerging risks for
the software business including 1) a potential increase in VMware customer churn in
2026/2027 as 3-year deals come up for renewal, 2) potential growth headwinds as the
VCF upsell is now being lapped, and 3) Al coding tools compressing code/app
modernization timelines and pushing more on-prem workload to the cloud. Net, we feel
like our assumption that AVGO's software business grows ~4-6% CAGR through 2029
still accounts for these risks. Lastly, given some of these headwinds and the growth from
the Al business, we believe the business synergy case for these two segments is less
attractive than it has been over the past several years and compute remains a hole where
AVGO could potentially allocate additional capital to fully flesh out its Al offerings
Software and semis seem to be on diverging trajectories...
VMware and the smaller software businesses alongside it have been thoroughly re-
engineered with substantial price increases (with VCF offering 'more-for-more'
compared to several disaggregated products under the old go-to-market strategy).
Combined with dramatic cost cuts, this has propelled AVGO's software business to
industry leading margins, but now sits atop a plateau with possible customer churn and
competitive pressure ahead. At the same time, the Al business continues to accelerate
with revenue set to triple this year to >560B in F2026 and ~$75B in C2026 with Al
revenue set to reach ~$120B in C2027 - essentially achieving the maximum PSU
threshold of ~1.8MM shares in the company's most recent CEO compensation
agreement.

The growth of AVGO's semiconductor business has taken off due to Al - mostly ASICS
but also networking. Most of this growth has come from hyperscalers and LLM
developers - nearly all of whom are working with AVGO on custom ASIC programs to
combat reliance on NVDA. The scope of the growth from these customers dwarfs what
is happening at the enterprise level, dulling the argument for revenue synergies in the
software business that really move the needle. It is true that software provides stable
cash flows, but AVGO's growth is being held back more than ever by the software
business, and compute remains a hole where AVGO could potentially allocate additional
capital to fully flesh out its Al offerings (major ASIC customers like Google bring the vast
majority of the compute IP to the ASIC engagement).
Even with software multiple compression, we struggle to characterize AVGO's
semis business as expensive
AVGO has been flattish YTD against a backdrop of the SOX returning +15% and
software -27% as the software vertical has now lost its long-term valuation premium to
the broader stock market (Figure 6). We update and revise our infrastructure software
peers for AVGO's software business, and we show pro forma software vs semiconductor
valuations (Figure 4). For this analysis, we burden a standalone software business with ~
$1B in cost dis-synergies - a number that we think is reasonable considering the $4B of
cost savings executed at the time of the merger but yet sufficient for some reinvestment
in R&D and - $250MM of public company costs. Backing out software EBITDA, P/E, and
FCF at the 12x, 19x, and 12x 2027 multiples of AVGO's software peers (see Figure 5 for
peer set), under these assumptions, the remaining semiconductor business currently
trades at a very modest premium of ~5% (or 1x turn) in relation to key peers on C2027E
Specifically, on EV/EBITDA the implied semiconductor valuation is ~ 17x '27E EBITDA (v5
peers at ~ 16x), ~20x P/E (v5 peers at ~ 19x), and ~ 23x EV/FCF (v5 peers at ~ 22x). In other
words, by any of these metrics, AVGO is trading just about one turn above its peer set
and certainly not misaligned with core peers like NVDA.
FQ1:26 (Jan) earnings preview (reports 3/4); visibility on GM impact from
Anthropic racks is the key Q
Given inflecting TPU units, we see AVGO's $8.2B Al revenue guidance for FQ1 as
conservative and also see Street ~ $9.3B for FQ2 as a few hundred millions low too. For
the non Al businesses, we view the -10% guidance as also a bit conservative even
accounting for normal seasonal weakness across wireless, broadband, and industrial.
We model Software down ~2% Q/Q, in line with guidance, as post-VMware customer
transitions approach the tail end and growth decelerates versus the past two years. Into
Apr Q, We expect ongoing TPU momentum (as outlined in our recent note) and Al
networking strength with Al revenues at ~59.7B (Street ~$9.3B). Combined with
seasonal recovery in non-Al semis we see AVGO guiding revenue at least to our ~ $20.8B
estimate - considerably above Street estimates. We see AVGO shipping ~3.7MM TPUS In
C2026, reflecting strength in the GCP program and incremental demand from
Anthropic (and potentially META as its ASIC program has we think been slow to really
reach critical mass). We believe the most important thing for the company to address on
this call is the margin impact from the Anthropic racks starting to ship in FQ3. The good
news is that even assuming these rack shipments are a ~500bps headwind to GM
between FQ1 and FQ4 of this year, we still see Street EPS as considerably too low - but
we think it would be beneficial for the company to provide some more color around how
to model this to remove some of the overhang for investors. Net, we maintain our C26/
C27 revenue estimates of $106B/$155B (9%/13% above consensus) and EPS of ~$11/
$17, reiterate our Buy rating, and maintain our $475 price target.
