Strong 2027 Outlook Signals the Party isn't Quite
Over
Strong Al outlook of over $100B in FY27 likely proves conservative with
expectations to supply 10GW of compute including 1GW+ to AVGO's 6th
announced customer OpenAl. The Al spend overhang will still linger but AVGO
made a strong case for their Al revenue to outgrow the market and see
continued growth through C28.
AVGO provided granular guidance for 10GWs of Al demand in FY27 including OpenAl of 1 GW+ and
3GW from Anthropic. Our math suggests revenue per GW was $13B/GW in CY25 rising to just under
$18B in CY26 and we would expect this to ramp to $20-25B/GW in 2027, implying potential for Al
revenue to actually reach over $200B. Networking also outgrowing ASICs, with networking up to
40% of Al revenue in the guided quarter vs. 1/3 in the current quarter and expected to continue
through CY27 where expectations are for 35-40% networking. AVGO pointing to Networking share
gains with dominance in 1.6T DSPs and TH6 (furthering next year with TH7 - 200T) as well as
higher attach rate as major drivers of the better Networking (we believe China likely a tailwind as
well). While GMs will blend down in the back half, the messaging on GM suggesting not as much
as feared even when sales to Anthropic start. Overall, the debate for AVGO (and NVDA) will still be
how sustainable cloud capex growth will be into C28 and this call didn't necessarily answer that
question, but this helps provide line of sight to $25-30 of EPS in CY27 (vs our prior $20 upside case)
and we struggle to see how these compute names will remain this cheap.
January Beat Driven by Continued Al Strength: January revenue came in better at $19.3B (+7%
Q/Q, vs St. $19.2B). Semiconductor Solutions reported above at $12.5B (+13% Q/Q, vs St. $12.3B)
with GM of 68% and OM of 60%. Within the segment, Al revenue reported slightly better than our
model at $8.4B (+31% Q/Q. vs our $8.3B). ASIC revenue made up 2/3 of Al revenue at ~$5.6B (+25%
Q/Q. vs our $5.8B) with Networking making up the other 1/3 of Al revenue at ~$2.8B (+43% Q/Q.
vs our $2.5B). Non-Al revenue reported in line with our model at $4.1B (-12% Q/Q). Within Non-Al.
Enterprise networking, broadband service, and storage revenues were up Y/Y, offset by seasonal
decline in wireless. Infrastructure Software came in below at $6.8B (-2% Q/Q, vs St. $7.0B) with GM
of 93% and OM of 78%. Consolidated GMs came in 10 bps ahead at 77.0% (vs St. 76.9%). Operating
margins came in 80 bps above at 66.4% (vs St. 65.6%) driven by higher revenue on top of lower
Opex at $2.0B (vs St. S2.2B). EBITDA margin came in 170 bps better at 68.0% (vs St. 66.3%) and
EPS slightly beat at $2.05 (vs St. $2.02).
April Guidance Well Ahead with Strong Al Networking Growth: April revenue guide came in well
above the Street at $22.0B (+14% Q/Q, vs St. $20.4B). Semiconductor Solutions revenue guided
well above at $14.8B (+12% O/Q. vs St. $13.1B) with Al revenue better at $10.7B (+27% Q/Q, vs our
$9.5B) and Non-Al revenue in line at $4.1B (Flattish Q/Q). Continued below.

Sustainability Matters
Top Material Issue: Product Design & Lifecycle: AVGO is a leader in infrastructure semiconductors and
software. Its product cycles, often launched ahead of competitors, help drive demand via power reduction
and increased system capabilities. Its data centers account for 1% of global power consumption today,
and this could rise to 13% by 2030. Networking in data centers accounts for ~9% of a data center's
consumption and is steadily growing. AVGO highlights that its co-packaged optics solutions roadmap,
introduced in 2023, delivers 4x the performance with 40% lower power per gigabit.
Company Targets: 1) Continue to meet the increased requirements for high-performance and low-power
products. 2) Reduce Scope 1 and Scope 2 GHG emissions by 38% by 2030 from 2021 baseline. Questions
for Management: 1) How is AVGO able to maintain its leadership in switching and routing? - what is
AVGO doing to maintain leadership? 2) How does your co-packaged optics strategy compare with the
approach taken by MRVL-IPHI and Intel-Barefoot? How much of the transceiver market do co-packaged
solutions replace?

Within Al, ASICs expected to make up 60% of revenue at ~$6.4B (+14% O/Q. vs our $6.6B) and
Networking to make up 40% at ~$4.3B (+54% Q/Q, vs our $2.8B). Infrastructure Software guided
slightly lower at $7.2B (+6% Q/Q, vs St. $7.3B). GM guide came in better at 77.0% (flat Q/Q. vs St. 76.7%)
along with EBITDA margin guidance at 68.0% (vs St. 67.3%). Tax expected at 16.5% for April and F26
due to impact of global minimum tax and shift in geo mix.
Notable Call Details: Delivered a decisive Al-driven beat and raise alongside materially stronger
multi-year visibility with Al chip revenue exceeding $100B in F27 alone (we see potentially $200B+).
Importantly, mgmt clarified this figure refers strictly to silicon content (XPUs, switch ASICs, DSPs).
excluding any system or rack-level revenue. Visibility is underpinned by deep, multi-year custom silicon
engagements with six customers (up from five), including Google (TPUs), Meta (MTIA), Anthropic, and
OpenAl. Aggregate XPU deployments indicated to approach 10GW in 2027 alone with content per GW
varying by customer architecture. GOOG's 7th-generation Ironwood TPU remains on a strong growth
trajectory through C26, with even higher demand expected for next-gen TPUs in C27+. Anthropic
deployments are tracking toward 1GW of compute in C26 with demand expected to surge beyond
3GW in C27. Meta's MTIA roadmap was explicitly defended as alive and well, with current shipments
underway and multi-GW scaling expected in C27 and beyond. Notably disclosed OpenAl as a sixth
XPU customer with volume deployments expected to begin in C27 at >1 GW. Customers 4/5 expected
to see strong shipments this year and expected to more than double in C27. Al networking strength
continues with strong demand for TH6 and TH7 expected to come in C27 with 2x the performance.
A key architectural theme was the ability to keep customers on Direct Attach Copper (DAC) longer
in scale-up within the rack, allowing customers to avoid premature transitions to CPO architectures
(framed CPO as a longer-dated transition which we agree with). Non-Al businesses remains stable and
reiterated that Al is additive to VMware demand. Authorized an additional $10B repurchase program
through C26. Inventory rose modestly as the company proactively secured wafers, HBM, substrates,
and T-glass to support Al growth, with capacity now locked through 2028.
