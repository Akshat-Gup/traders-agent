Broadcom Inc. (AVGO): Very strong guidance and commentary on key
debates should drive stock higher - Buy
Key stock takeaways: We expect the stock to trade meaningfully higher following
guidance that came in well above the Street, as well as management commentary
that we believe should address several key investor debates on the stock. First,
Broadcom's incremental business visibility has led the company to guide FY27 AI
semiconductor revenue significantly in excess of $ 100bn across up to 10GW of
datacenter deployments - which we believe was a material upward revision to
investor expectations. Second, management provided additional clarity on its
custom silicon engagements beyond Google - namely Meta, Anthropic, and OpenAl -
which are all expected to ramp into FY27. Third, management noted that it has
secured supplies of all key components needed to support its revenue forecast
through FY28 - and no longer expects its margins to be diluted by increasing rack
level shipments in the coming quarters. We remain Buy rated (on CL) as we believe
Broadcom's leadership in Al networking and custom silicon enables the lowest
inference cost for its hyperscaler customers, and we see it delivering ongoing cost
reductions on pace with market leader Nvidia (Buy).
Quarterly results were in line with the Street: Broadcom reported revenue of
$19.3 bn, in line with GS at $19.3 bn and the Street at $19.2 bn. Gross margin of
77.0% was just above GS at 76.9% and the Street at 76.8%. Operating margin of
66.4% was above GS at 66.0% and the Street at 65.5%. Operating EPS of $2.05 was
just below GS at $2.11 but above the Street at $2.02. Al Semiconductor revenue
grew 106% YoY to $8.4bn, and compares to GS at $8.3 bn and the Street at $8.2 bn.
Semiconductor Solutions revenue of $12.5 bn was in line with GS at $12.5 bn and just
above the Street at $12.3 bn. Infrastructure Software revenue of $6.8 bn was slightly
below GS and the Street at $6.9 bn.
• Al semiconductor revenue outlook: Broadcom continues to see accelerating
demand for its Al semiconductor solutions across both networking and XPUs.
Based on increased visibility and backlog from multiple customers (see below),
Broadcom expects that it can deliver Al semiconductor revenue significantly in
excess of $100 bn in FY27 across up to 10GW of datacenter deployments, which
we believe is a material positive to revisions to Street and investor expectations.
The company also noted that it expects to grow in FY28 off this elevated base.
Our revised Al semiconductor revenue estimates now stand at
$60bn/$130bn/$170bn for FY26/27/28.

Broadcom announced that its engagement with Google for its 7th generation TPU

solution to deploy 1GW in FY27.
• Supply chain assurance: Having anticipated significant potential shortages across a
wide range of components (memory, lasers, packaging, etc.), Broadcom announced
that it has been able to secure all of the components it needs to support its revenue
forecast through FY28. We believe this is a significant positive as it addresses a key
investor concern regarding potential gross margin erosion across the Al
semiconductor space.
• Margins: In contrast to prior management commentary regarding the potentially
dilutive impact of full-rack systems to Broadcom's margins, the company now
believes that it can absorb this impact given increased scale of production and
improving yields on its Al products. In addition, we believe the company's
constructive commentary regarding component supply should help Broadcom
maintain its current margin structure on its Al products in the medium term.
• Networking strength: Broadcom continues to see very strong deployments of its
leading Ethernet technology across a wide range of customers in both scale-out and
scale-up applications. Its switch products continue to lead the market with
Tomahawk 6, and the company expects to see a very strong ramp with its
forthcoming Tomahawk 7 product. The company continues to see its SERDES
leadership driving copper-based networking solutions for 1.6 Tb/s and beyond. In
addition, within Al semiconductors, the company now expects an increased
contribution from its networking products - with 40% contribution expected in 2Q
and 33%-40% contribution expected in the coming quarters (up from ~ 30% prior).
2Q guidance is well above the Street, driven by Al strength. Broadcom guided 2Q
revenue and adjusted EBITDA margin above the Street. Revenue was guided to $22.0 bn,
which is above GS at $20.4 bn and the Street at $20.5 bn. Al Semiconductor revenue was
guided to $10.7 bn, which is well above GS and the Street at $9.3 bn. Adjusted EBITDA
margin guidance of 68% was above GS at 67.2% and the Street at 67.1%.
Estimate changes. We significantly increase our estimates to reflect higher Al revenue
relative to our previous assumptions, consistent with company guidance (see detailed
estimates below).
