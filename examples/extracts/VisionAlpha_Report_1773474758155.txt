Broadcom Inc (AVGO US)
Buy: Potent mix of ASIC and networking upside
• Significant FY26e Al revenue upside driven by ASIC
Vision Alphal
momentum and networking TAM expansion
• Sizable upside to street estimates; our FY26e EPS relatively
in line with consensus' FY27e EPS
• Reinstate Broadcom (Buy) as our top Al semiconductor pick; TP
+34% to USD535 and FY26-27e EPS +17-30%, both street highs
ASIC momentum unstoppable even without OpenAl: We believe the street is still
undervaluing the ASIC opportunity despite revising up FY26e/FY27e ASIC revenue
estimates by 47%/60% since 3QFY25 results. We believe AVGO has increased its
FY26e CoWoS capacity allocation significantly from 150k wafers to 250k wafers.
Alphabet (GOOGL US) continues to be the main driver for the incremental revision as
we now expect Google tensor processing unit (TPU) shipments of 3.1m (from 1.7m)
in FY26e. We now expect Meta ASIC shipments of 900k (from 750k) units driven by
ramp of its Athena and Iris chips. We believe that the fourth customer announced is
Anthropic and expect cUSD5bn ASIC revenue contribution in FY26e. Hence, we lift
FY26e/FY27e ASIC revenue +46%/+63% to USD44.7bn/USD72.0bn, now 44%/59%
above Visible Alpha consensus of USD30.9bn/USD45.4bn. The OpenAl deal (10GW
deployment) lacks clarity on the part Broadcom plays. However, if Broadcom does
the full design of the chip, we estimate an ASIC revenue opportunity close to
USD14bn per GW deployment with upside to networking revenue too
Networking can drive further upside driven by TAM expansion: The overall Al
total addressable market (TAM) is expanding beyond traditional hyperscalers into
emerging hyperscalers, enterprise and sovereign Al, driving the Al networking TAM
higher too. Already the market leader in Ethernet switches, Broadcom has also
matched digital signal processor (DSP) market leader Marvell's roadmap with its 1.6T
solution (see Marvell Technology (MRVL US): Initiate at Hold, 24 November 2025).
Therefore, we believe Al networking could provide an earnings upside surprise.
Hence, our FY26e/FY27e Al networking revenue estimates are 8%/11% above
consensus. Our bull case scenario with FY26e Al networking revenue growth of 100%
y-o-y (vs base case 50% y-o-y) implies further 4% upside to our base case EPS.
Our FY26e EPS almost at par with consensus' FY27e: ASIC strength and the
networking upside opportunity can drive significant upside to consensus FY26e EPS
estimates. We believe that the upside opportunity is so significant that consensus
FY27e EPS can be achieved in FY26e. Our FY26e EPS of USD11.68 is just 5%
below consensus FY27e EPS of USD12.31.
Reiterate Buy; lift TP to USD535 (from USD400): Our USD535 TP is derived by
applying a FY27 target PE of 32x (from 31x) to our FY27e EPS of USD16.72 (from
USD12.85). With c51% implied upside, we retain a Buy rating on the stock. We
believe the upside to ASIC and Al networking revenue can help AVGO re-rate
towards its peak FY27e PE of 32x.

Broadcom reinstated as our top Al semiconductor pick
We reinstate Broadcom as our top Al semiconductor pick amongst our coverage driven by the
following factors:
We're seeing strong demand from Alphabet for its TPU - now both for internal as well as
external use. Meta is also expected to ramp its ASIC chips next year. As a result, we expect the
CoWoS capacity for FY26e to expand from 150k wafer to 250k wafers driving our revised
FY26e/FY27e ASIC revenue estimates to be 44%/59% above consensus.
• We believe the overall AI TAM expansion beyond traditional hyperscalers will drive networking
TAM higher as well. Hence, we believe the street is undervaluing Al networking revenue. Our
FY26e/FY27e Al networking revenue estimates are 8%/11% higher than the street.
The combined strength from ASIC and networking gives us conviction that Broadcom can
deliver a significant upside surprise to street expectations. Hence, our FY26e/FY27e EPS
estimates are the highest on the street - 24%/36% higher than consensus.
Additionally, we believe that Broadcom can deliver consensus' FY27e EPS estimates in
FY26e as our FY26e EPS estimate of USD11.68 is just 5% below consensus FY27e EPS
estimate of USD12.31.
We expect Broadcom to have higher FY27e revenue growth of 35% vs the average of 27%
for Al accelerator plays. We estimate Broadcom to grow earnings by 43% in FY27e vs the
peer group average of 30%. Despite better growth expectations, it is trading at a 21x
FY2Te PE, virtually aligned with the peer group average of 20x. Hence, we believe the risk-
reward is very enticing.

ASIC momentum going from strength to strength
In our 29 August note (Broadcom: No ST earnings surprise but remains our top pick, we had
highlighted that the street was undervaluing the ASIC potential and had flagged the CoWoS
capacity expansion as well as pricing premium. Our FY26e/FY27e ASIC revenue estimates were
49%/59% higher. There has been significant consensus upward revision since then driven by the
announcement of a fourth ASIC customer as well as the OpenAl deal. However, we believe the
story remains the same— the ASIC potential remains significantly undervalued by the street.
Recent developments have driven street estimates higher, but still not enough
We've seen significant revision to ASIC revenue estimates since 3QFY25 financial results driven by
• the announcement of a new ASIC customer, Broadcom's fourth, which would add an
additional USD10bn in Al revenue in FY26e;
and announcement of a deal with OpenAl on 13 October under which 10GW of systems
that include accelerators and Ethernet solutions, co-designed by OpenAl and Broadcom
would be deployed
Since 3QFY25 results, the street has revised its FY26e/FY27e ASIC revenue estimates by
47%/60% to USD30.9bn/USD45.4bn respectively. Primarily driven by this, the
FY26e/FY27e EPS estimates have seen an upward revision of 13%/23%.
Despite the significant upward estimate revisions highlighted above, we believe the street is still
undervaluing the ASIC revenue opportunity for Broadcom. The street's current ASIC revenue
estimates for FY26e/FY27e of USD30.9bn/USD45.4bn are now in line with our old estimates of
USD30.6bn/USD44.1bn.
